The data is a ski resort parking area — that explains the yellow (bare ground/pavement) and pink/purple (vegetation in CIR). 
The circular arc patterns we saw are the LiDAR scan lines from the state's 1m resolution dataset.
The current rendering issues make sense now:

The dataset is very small geographically (a parking lot)
The state LiDAR at 1m resolution means points are spaced 1m apart — sparse for a close-up 360 view
The CIR colors look "wrong" because infrared makes vegetation pink/purple and bare ground yellow — that's correct, just unfamiliar

The Structure from Motion point cloud would be much better for this because:

Drone imagery gives ~2-3cm resolution vs 1m for state LiDAR
SfM generates a dense mesh of millions of tightly packed points
The colors would be natural RGB, not infrared
It would actually look like a place you're standing in

The current 360 rendering isn't "wrong" per se — it's just that 1m LiDAR of a flat parking lot isn't visually interesting from ground level.
The gaps and black areas are literally where the laser didn't hit. My recommendation: ask for the SfM point cloud. It will render dramatically better.
